★ Steps marked with this tag are safe for the kids to help. 5yo and 3yo can both do starred steps with a little supervision.
Phase 1 Setup 5 min
1
Park at curb, stage equipment
Run the power cord along the curb edge, out of the road.
Keep kids on the sidewalk side during setup.
✓
2
Fill two buckets
Bucket 1: water + Slick-N-Slide. Bucket 2: clean water only. Label them.
★ kids — can help pour soap into Bucket 1
Always rinse mitt in Bucket 2 before re-dipping in Bucket 1. This prevents scratches.
✓
3
Load soap blaster
Attach to wand. Fill with diluted Slick-N-Slide (blaster ratio — more diluted than the bucket).
Green nozzle (25°) on paint only. Never use the red nozzle on the car.
✓
Phase 2 Exterior 25 min
4
Pre-rinse — no soap
Green nozzle, 12–18" from paint. Roof → sides → bumpers → wheel wells → sliding door tracks.
Sliding door tracks get the most grime — linger there. Full 3 min.
✓
5
Soap blast — full car
Coat entire car top-to-bottom in foam. Let sit 2–3 min. Don't let it dry.
Work in shade. While foam dwells → go to Step 6.
✓
6
Wheels & tires (while foam dwells)
Wheel brush + soap bucket. Scrub face, lug holes, tire sides. Rinse each with green nozzle.
Never use the wash mitt on wheels — it will scratch the paint later.
✓
7
Rinse foam off
Green nozzle, top-to-bottom. Keep moving — don't hold still on one spot.
Still see suds? Keep rinsing.
✓
8
Wash with mitt
Roof → hood → sides top to bottom → bumpers last. Straight lines only, no circles.
Rinse mitt in Bucket 2 after every panel, reload from Bucket 1.
✓
9
Final rinse
Top-to-bottom. Crack windows, rinse door tops. Don't forget door jambs.
Check: no soap suds anywhere.
✓
10
Dry
Clean microfiber, top-down. Pat or drag — no scrubbing. Open each door, dry the sills.
★ kids — can dry lower doors and bumpers with their own towel
Work fast in sun — water spots form quickly.
✓
Phase 3 Interior 18 min
11
Pull & clean all-weather mats — all 3 rows
Shake out, spray both sides with green nozzle, lean against car to dry.
★ kids — can shake out the rear mats
Don't reinstall until fully dry.
✓
12
Vacuum — all 3 rows
Crevice tool in seat seams. Brush on fabric first. Seats forward → rear floor. Seats back → front floor.
★ kids — 5yo can vacuum the third-row floor
Check under second-row seats — that's where snacks disappear.
✓
13
Vacuum vents
Detailing brush flicks dust out, vacuum right behind it.
One quick pass per vent.
✓
14
Leather seats — clean
Apply cleaner to cloth (not directly on seat). Small sections, wipe off clean. All rows.
Focus on seat bolsters (sides). Don't skip rear seats even if they look fine.
✓
15
Leather seats — condition
Thin coat on a clean cloth. Work in, wait 2 min, buff lightly with dry microfiber.
Less is more. Do the steering wheel too.
✓
16
Wipe hard surfaces
Damp microfiber on dash, console, door panels, sliding door rails, cup holders.
★ kids — can wipe lower door panels and cup holders
Spray the cloth, not the screen.
✓
17
Interior glass
Invisible Glass on microfiber. Up-down strokes, then side-to-side. Buff off haze with dry towel.
★ kids — can wipe the rear side windows
Do in shade. Sliding door windows get the most kid handprints — extra passes there.
✓
Phase 4 Finishing 7 min
18
Ceramic spray — exterior paint
Car must be dry. 2–3 pumps on microfiber. Wipe one panel, flip towel, buff off within 60 sec. Roof → hood → sides → bumpers.
Skip wheels, rubber trim, and glass. Work in shade.
✓
19
Exterior glass
Invisible Glass on microfiber. All windows + mirrors. Buff dry.
Windshield corners collect wiper residue — extra attention there.
✓
20
Reinstall mats
Only once fully dry. Clip into anchors in each row.
Still damp? Leave out 15 more min.
✓
21
Walkthrough + cleanup
Walk all the way around. Wipe door jambs before closing. Drain buckets, coil cord.
★ kids — can drain buckets and put away towels
Done.
